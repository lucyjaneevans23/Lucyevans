Leeds City Library is a beloved institution in the heart of West Yorkshire, for this University project I was instructed to model a re-brand to modernise their image to match it's evolving role in the community. The goal of this project was to develop a modern, engaging brand identity that would resonate with a diverse range of users, engage with a new audience and reflect the library's values of accessibility, creativity, and social responsibility.
Logo Guidelines

Logo

Logo2

The existing brand identity of Leeds City Library was dated and lacked consistency across all touchpoints. The logo, typography, and color palette were not easily recognizable or memorable, making it difficult to differentiate the library from other cultural institutions in the city. Moreover, the brand lacked a clear tone of voice and visual identity that reflected its mission and values.

I conducted extensive research to understand the needs and aspirations of the library. I analyzed the competitive landscape, identified opportunities for differentiation, and developed a comprehensive strategy to rebrand the library.

The rebranded identity for Leeds City Library is now more modern, engaging, and recognizable. The new logo is iconic and memorable, while the color palette and typography are consistent across all touchpoints. The tone of voice is friendly and inclusive, reflecting the library's commitment to serving all members of the community. Hopefully, to increase visibility and credibility for the library, enhancing its reputation as a hub for knowledge, creativity, and social connection in Leeds.
